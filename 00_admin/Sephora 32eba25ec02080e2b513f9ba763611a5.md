### Company Overview

- Sephora is the world's largest beauty retailer, founded in 1969 in Limoges, France
- Joined LVMH Group in 1997
- Operates in 35 markets across North America, Europe, and Asia
- Over 80 million active loyalty cardholders worldwide who purchased at least once in the last year
- 320 students (interns and apprentices) at headquarters, including Lully from Albert School
- 190 nationalities represented among employees, with 48% under 30 years old

### Sephora's Magic Formula

- **Experience**: Focus on in-store retail experience and flagship mobile app available 24/7
- **Product Selection**: Three categories - Sephora Collection (own brand), exclusive brands (only at Sephora), and selective brands like Guerlain and Dior
- **Communities**: Largest beauty community with over 80 million loyalty members and new "My Sephora" rewards program launched
- **Talent**: Strong sense of belonging with over 80% employee engagement

### Data Challenge Context

- 80 million clients making multiple purchases annually with several products per purchase creates massive data volume
- More than 350 brands distributed in France
- Teams struggle with workload managing transactional data, customer behavior, emotions, and satisfaction
- Key challenge is making data actionable and reducing time to activation
- Without action, millions in data infrastructure investment "burns to ashes"

### Three Case Studies Presented

**Case 1: Customer Journey Analysis** 

- Understand how customers evolve through their Sephora journey
- Identify drivers creating high value customers
- Example challenge: determine which customer is more loyal - one spending €50 once per year vs. one spending €25 three times per year

**Case 2: Advanced Customer Segmentation** 

- Address complexity in database and improve customer segmentation
- Use machine learning with clustering and instrumentation
- Expected outcomes: improved personalization, targeted video offers, prioritized hyper-transfer segments
- Focus on KPIs for business value

**Case 3: Brand Affinity Detection** 

- Identify brands with highest adoption potential for cross-selling
- Analyze which brands or customers look similar to each other
- Explore brand co-occurrence in baskets and customer profile similarities
- Consider correlation vs. causality carefully
- Business value: increase average basket size, accelerate brand discovery, enable product recommendations

### Dataset Details

- Sample contains approximately 65,000 customers (small sample from 80 million total)
- France-only data from 2025
- Sample is representative despite small size compared to full database

### Dataset Overview

- Contains approximately 65,000 customers from France (including Luxembourg and Monaco)
- Data covers 2025 only
- Dataset is anonymized with randomized customer IDs
- Includes high-frequency customers to enable customer journey analysis
- Same dataset provided for all three case studies, so teams should filter for relevant columns

### Key Dataset Variables Explained

- **Fidelity status**: Customer tier (bronze, silver, gold) that can change over time with purchases
- **RFM segment ID**: Recency, Frequency, and Amount segmentation used at Sephora
- **Customer attributes**: Age category, generation (Gen Z, Gen A, etc.), gender, subscription date, city
- **Transaction data**: Ticket ID, transaction date, sales before VAT in euros, discounts (manual or automatic), quantity
- **Store information**: Store code, name, city, and channel (in-store, online, click & collect indicated by empty star)
- **Product details**: Material code, axis (makeup, skincare, fragrance, haircare, other/services), market description (selective, exclusive, Sephora Collection), brand
- **First purchase data**: First purchase date, channel of recruitment (store, web, or app), and all related transaction details

### Product Categories Clarification

- **Sephora Collection**: Sephora's own brand with highest margins
- **Exclusive brands**: Only sold at Sephora (e.g., Fenty, Hailey Bieber's brand), though brands may also sell on their own websites
- **Selective brands**: Distributed brands like Chanel and Guerlain
- Margins and business value differ significantly between categories

### Recommended Methodology for Data Projects

**Initial Steps:**

- Define business context and scope first before diving into data
- Understand objectives and desired outcomes rather than asking business stakeholders for specific solutions
- Organize project structure (folders, documentation) before analysis
- Explore dataset structure, column types, value ranges, and data quality

**Analysis Process:**

- Perform data quality checks and remove unnecessary columns
- Create new KPIs and calculated fields (feature engineering)
- Calculate metrics like average basket per customer and purchase frequency
- Conduct correlation and causality analysis
- Apply machine learning methods as appropriate
- Iterate on analysis - definition of "done" is often constrained by time and budget

**Important Considerations:**

- Don't narrow creativity too early by jumping straight to AI/ChatGPT for solutions
- Avoid asking business stakeholders for solutions - they think within their current framework
- Focus on business value, not just obvious patterns (e.g., expensive items sold together)
- Work with quantities in addition to revenue to avoid misleading conclusions from high-priced items

### Success Metrics & Business Context

- Success can be defined as: increased basket size, higher quantities, more diverse products per basket
- Teams should propose their own definition of success based on analysis
- Business objectives include: increasing average basket value, accelerating brand discovery, improving personalization and recommendations
- Affinity scores will be used for e-merchandising, CRM actions, in-app product feeds, and store objectives

### Key Questions Addressed

- **No NDA required**: Dataset is anonymized and randomized, safe to use with AI tools despite PC limitations
- **No minimum purchase requirement**: Dataset includes all customer frequencies, though analysis may require setting thresholds
- **Brand prioritization**: Teams should explore and propose recommendations - no predetermined brand priorities
- **External research**: Teams can research competitors and external data for comparison, though primary focus should be on provided dataset
- **Benchmark figures**: Sephora has internal brand adoption rates but won't share due to dataset being a random sample
