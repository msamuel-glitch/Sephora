What Lift Means

A lift score measures purely incremental affinity. A 65.4x score (as seen with AVÈNE to BIODERMA) means the rule correctly predicts a transition 65.4 times more effectively than random baseline targeting. It is the purest signal of unprompted discovery intent.

The Recommendation Engine

Four Candidate Routes

  1. Basket Co-occurrence: Brands consistently purchased together with significant lift.
  2. Collaborative Filtering: Recommendations based on structural similarities between behavioral twins.
  3. Content-Based: Extracting untested brands within a customer's dominant axis.
  4. Cold-Start Proxy: Bootstrapping emerging brands by mapping them to validated proxy audiences.
Holdout Validation Methodology

The model was trained strictly on data from January to September 2025. It was then validated against a blind holdout window containing out-of-sample data from October to December 2025 to prove generalized accuracy.

The Behavioral Transition Model

A foundational analysis parsing 224,212 observed transitions into 894 actionable behavioural rules.

Headline Finding 1

Fragrance triples basket value. Every single customer converted to Fragrance triples their transaction value across all generation brackets.

Headline Finding 2

SEPHORA is a bridge. It acts universally as a rebound brand stabilizing the transition gap between two premium prestige brand purchases, rather than functioning as a final destination brand.

Headline Finding 3

Diversity drives retention. Brand diversity correlates stronger with long-term retention metrics than brand depth. Multi-brand exploration directly equates to customer loyalty.

The Male Fragrance Anomaly

While men make up just 6% of the base, their average spend is the highest in the dataset (€52.44), driven explicitly by Fragrance which constitutes 69% of their revenue at an astonishing €95.98 average basket. They enter via Fragrance, and they stay there.

The Protection Layer

This is the only system framework in this structural class that actively formalizes both a reward function (highest propensity) and a cost function (maximum risk). Knowing when not to recommend is equally critical to relationship equity.
